Course Description

In modern physics and engineering, computation is essential for planning, modeling, visualization, and analysis. It provides viable means—sometimes the only viable means—to make sense of what is going on.

This course provides a broad, interdisciplinary survey of computational tools and methods. Unlike a traditional computer science course, our focus is on the practical application of existing tools, algorithms, and platforms to solve complex, real-world problems. We will blitz through several topics, giving you the skills to dig deep and build amazing things.

We will cover the following modules:

  • Python: General coding, data analysis, visualization, and algos.
  • COMSOL: To solve ODE/PDEs, Finite Element Analysis (FEA), and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD).
  • MD Simulations: A dive into Molecular Dynamics.
  • Digital Signals: Working with wearable biosensors, data collection, and motion detection.
  • Final Project: A multi-week project to showcase your new skills.

Course Philosophy

Grading: The Learning Loop

This course uses a mastery-based "learning loop." There is no partial credit. Assignments are evaluated as Exemplary, Success, or Retry. The goal is to try, make mistakes, reflect, revise, and try again—without penalty for mistakes, as long as you demonstrate growth.

Tokens

You will start with 5 tokens. One token can be spent to get a 36-hour deadline extension or to retry an assignment a second time (a third submission).

AI Policy: "Roller Skates, Not Crutches"

AI tools (like ChatGPT or Copilot) are permitted as long as they support your learning and do not replace your independent thought. All AI-generated content (code or text) must be clearly cited and labeled.
